EVVO Snowshoes is a French brand with patented lightweight technology,
bio-sourced materials and a Michelin partnership. Award-winning products,
and a site that read like a technical catalogue: dense specs, a buried
value proposition and navigation built for engineers, not for people who
shop on feel.

<p class="note">Invisible innovation</p>

## Premium gear that couldn't say why

The research showed a simple disconnect: users could not tell why EVVO was
premium. Only 1 in 3 understood the sustainability angle, and 70% of
mobile traffic bounced off a desktop-first experience. The innovation was
buried under jargon. The job: make the shop sell the story and make
choosing a product quick and confident.

## From specs to story

Three findings set the strategy. The differentiators, Michelin soles and
recycled materials, were invisible in the copy. There was no responsive
design, while most research happened on mobile, against dense cards,
unclear sizing and no comparison tools. And interviews kept saying the
same thing: shoppers wanted comfort, quality and sustainability, not
feature tables.

We replaced the spaghetti of technical categories with a linear finder
flow. Need-based questions instead of a catalogue: less decision fatigue,
a shorter path to checkout.

## Need-based navigation

Eight technical SKU codes became activity-based categories, so users
self-segment instantly. Product cards follow the same logic: image first,
benefit one-liner second, spec last. Skim for value, dig for proof.

## Impact and reflections

The hypothesis: lead with story, support with specs. In usability testing
people understood the product faster and chose with more confidence.

Conversion needs a live shop to prove it. The judgement stands either way:
people don't buy a spec sheet, they buy the reason it exists, and every
screen now leads with that reason.
