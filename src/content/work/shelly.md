Your streak broke. So now you feel like shit. Shelly is a routine-building
app that refuses to work that way.

The client, the Daily Health Conference, is a non-profit promoting
wellness since 1983. The expertise is real; the technology lagged, and
memberships dropped with it. They needed a modern offer that keeps their
community, in a crowded health-tech space, strictly inside GDPR.

<p class="note">The 'no time' barrier</p>

## The streak punishes real life

City life leaves no room for rigid wellness plans. Research with 16
participants showed the conflict:

<div class="stats">
  <div><span>15 of 16</span><span>want more physical activity</span></div>
  <div><span>10 of 16</span><span>cite mental health or stress as their top concern</span></div>
  <div><span>4 of 16</span><span>cite work as the main barrier to routine</span></div>
</div>

Existing apps punish the slip: miss a streak and the app judges you, so
people leave. And a majority prioritise mental wellbeing over physical
metrics.
The answer became a tortoise philosophy: slow, steady, flexible habits.

## Impact and reflections

In usability testing, every participant completed the core flow and all
responded positively. The market is a harder judge, but the bet those
sessions validated is the one the whole product stands on: an app that
forgives the miss keeps people moving.

> Finally an app that feels like a friend.

The line above came from a participant in the final round of usability
tests.
